Tenses : Present Perfect Continuous and Past Perfect Continuous Tense

Present Perfect Continuous Tense

  • Description: The present perfect continuous tense describes actions that started in the past and are still continuing in the present or have recently stopped, but with a focus on the duration.
  • Structure: have/has been + verb (ing form)
  • Example:
    • "She has been studying for three hours."
      (The action began in the past and is still happening or has just finished.)

Past Perfect Continuous Tense

  • Description: The past perfect continuous tense describes actions that were ongoing in the past for some time before another past event occurred.
  • Structure: had been + verb (ing form)
  • Example:
    • "She had been studying for three hours before the exam started."
      (The action was happening before a specific point in the past.)

Key Difference:

  • Present Perfect Continuous is ongoing or just finished with relevance to the present.
  • Past Perfect Continuous occurred before another past event and has no relevance to the present.
